Dr. Lin Chen earned her Ph.D. in Psychology from South China Normal University. She served as a Research Associate at the Learning Research and Development Center at the University of Pittsburgh. Before moving to the U.S., she was a tenured associate professor position at Sun Yat-sen University in China. Outside of academia, Dr. Chen enjoys traveling, hiking, running, and reading.

Graduate Student

Emily Hall

ekhall2@illinois.edu

Emily Hall is a PhD student whose research focuses on neurodiverse readers. She integrates her experience as a literacy specialist and former middle school educator with the goal of making research on the neural basis of reading more accessible and practical. She is interested in how immersive reading technology can enhance readers’ critical thinking and deep reading skills. Specifically, her research focuses on creating inclusive EdTech solutions in Kenya to increase accessibility to the Science of Reading.
